2         Introduction

 

This chapter describes the negotiation mechanism that AlphaLinc provides to its users conducting business negotiations with others networked companies.

 

3         Overview

 

Using the negotiation facilities the user may send business messages to customer or suppliers companies. The negotiation process is a sequence of actions taken by the users driven by their businesses decisions. An action is enabled depending on the negotiation context. An action may change the negotiation status. The understanding this process by the user is relevant since AlphaLinc will display information regarding the negotiation based on the process situation.

 

The actions that may be taken by the user during a negotiation are:

 

  • Send
  • Create Answer
  • Close Deal
  • Final Offer
  • Cancel
  • Cancel All

 

The offer is the unit of information exchanged among negotiating companies. An offer contains information about the negotiation’s conditions and items. So, the items that are in process of negotiation are associated to the offer, and also, the payment conditions.

 

When a user wants to send a quotation for another company, it creates an offer. Then, he may add line items to that offer and, eventually, also adds installments. When the offer is ready, he may send it. A unique identifier identifies each offer created. The first offer is called the initial-offer. This type of offer may be sent to multiple companies. Each one will receive a non-editable copy of it, as a mirror offer.

 

When an offer reaches the target company, it receives a unique identifier specific to the target company. By its turn, the target company may take the following actions: Create Answer, Close Deal and Cancel. 

If the offer recipient decides to respond to the received offer, he clicks on Create Answer. A new editable offer is created of a counter-offer type. If he chooses Close Deal or Cancel, a new offer of the correspondent type is created locally and also at the target company.

 

 

Each possible action and its effects are detailed on the following items.

 

4         Negotiation Actions

 

4.1      Send

 

This action is enabled at offers that are on the in-conception state. There are two situations where it happens: after the creation of an initial-offer and after the creation of a counter-offer, resulting from the clicking of the Create Answer button, as will be seen later.

 

This action transfers the offer, the line items and installments definitions to the company or companies specified at the From/To tab fields.  So, each target company will receive a complete copy of origin offer, actually a mirror offer. The copied offer identifier is different, since it is a unique id at the target company itself.

 

For example, if an offer with number 000215 is sent to a group of suppliers, each supplier will receive and see that offer with a different number. So one supplier will receive that offer with number 000120, another may receive with number 000300, and so on.

 

The sending of an initial offer to multiple companies starts multiple parallel negotiation threads.

 

 

The figure above shows the sending of an initial offer to multiple companies, referred to at the From/To tab fields. Note that although all offers are exactly equal, each one has its own id (X, Y, Z, W).

 

A received offer is not editable, being kept unchangeable in the system. This way, it is available any time for reference or if a negotiation historic review is necessary.

 

The process of transferring an offer to a target company involves the transfer of the offer itself and also the line items and installments. If any of those transfers fails, an error message is showed and the transfer is interrupted. If the offer is created but any of its elements aren’t, the mirror offer is kept on the inactive state at the target company, being not available to the user.

4.2      Create Answer

 

This action is enabled for a received initial or counter offer.

 

This action creates a new editable offer to be used as a response to the received one. This new offer is created as a copy of the received one, with all fields copied. Some fields are allowed to be changed but others aren’t. It is created with in-conception status and of counter-offer type and can be simply removed by clicking on the trash icon without affecting the current negotiation or others offers. After its edition, the offer may be sent to the target company by clicking on SEND button.

 

The previous figure shows the result of the Create Answer button.

 

4.3      Close Deal

 

This action is enabled for the following received offer types: initial, final, counter and request-to-close offers.

 

This action has two different results, depending on the situation. It may result on the negotiation being closed or on the sending of a request to close deal to the target company.

 

If the company that initiated the negotiation presses the Closed Deal button, the result is the negotiation being closed. It means that a close offer is locally created and sent to the target company. Both offers contain exactly the same values, preserving the conditions accorded during the negotiation.

 

If the Closed Deal button is pressed by the company that originally received an initial-offer, a request-to-close offer is locally created and sent to the source company. A local copy of the request-to-close-offer is kept and a mirror offer is created at the source company. So, a company that is the receipt of an initial-offer never closes the deal, it can only request the closing of it. It is up to the company that created the negotiation to close it, whenever it decides to do it. So, the source company may close the deal, as it receives the request-to-close-offer. If it does so, a closed-deal-offer is created at both sides of the communication.

 

The closing of a negotiation thread doesn’t affect the parallel negotiation threads that may be running, resulting from the same initial offer.

4.4      Cancel

 

This action is always enabled for an offer of an active negotiation except for an own initial offer, because for the initial offer itself there is the Cancel All button. An active negotiation means one that is not yet cancelled, closed or expired.

 

This action results on the creation of a local cancel offer and a mirror at the target company.

 

A cancel interrupts the negotiation, and frees the associated line items.

 

 

 

 

The figure above shows the cancel being generated by the same company that sent the initial offer. To note the cancel offer being created at sides of the communications.

 

4.5      Cancel All

 

This action is enabled for an initial-offer but only for the company that originated it.

 

This action results on the sending of a cancel offer for all the negotiation threads associated with the initial-offer. Actually, a cancel is sent for each target company that is specified at the initial-offer, even if no counter offer have been received from the target company.

 

A cancel-all interrupts the negotiation threads, and frees the associated line items.

 

The figure below shows a cancel all being sent to all target companies. All receive a cancel offer even if a counter offer has already been sent.

 

4.6      Final Offer

 

This action is enabled for offers that have already been sent by the company. Its purpose is to enable the re-sending of an offer when no answer is received. The target company, when receives a final-offer, can only cancel it or do a close deal. Actually, for the receiving company, it is its last opportunity to continue the negotiation. If the target company decides to close the deal, the rules of the Close Deal action apply here. If  the company that decides to close the deal is the one that received the initial offer, then the close deal results in the sending of a request-to-close offer to the source company.

 

A Final Offer action results on the creation of a final offer at the sending company and a mirror offer at the target company.
